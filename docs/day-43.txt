Day 43: Store Assets, Privacy Compliance & App Submission
===================================================================
Goal: Finalize visual store assets, complete privacy/compliance declarations, and formally submit the StreakSky app to the Apple App Store and Google Play Store for review.

This day is critical for the final launch preparation. The focus shifts from coding to marketing, compliance, and navigating the complex app submission processes. Below is a detailed explanation of each task for interview preparation.

1. Prepare Store Screens (Task 127)
-----------------------------------
Your App Store screenshots are the most important conversion tool you have. Users decide whether to download your app within 3-5 seconds of looking at these images.

- What we did:
  - Created 8 high-resolution screenshots for both iOS (6.5" and 5.5" display sizes) and Android (Phone and 7-inch Tablet).
  - Adopted the "Neon Aesthetic" theme (#0D0D0D backgrounds with #B3FF00 accents). We placed the app screens inside sleek, minimalistic device frames.
  - Added short, punchy typography above each screen to highlight core value propositions:
    1. "Build Unbreakable Habits" (Home Screen with Habit Cards)
    2. "See Your Consistency" (GitHub-style Heatmap)
    3. "Sky AI: Your Personal Coach" (Chat UI)
    4. "Track Your Career Goals" (Phase Timeline)
  - Designed the mandatory 1024x500 Feature Graphic for the Google Play Store, serving as a banner at the top of our listing.

  *Interview Tip*: Be prepared to explain how you generated these assets. Mention tools like Figma for design, or automated tools like Fastlane Frameit. Highlight that screenshots should tell a "story" of the user journey rather than just showing random screens.

2. Setup Privacy Labels and Data Usage Disclosure (Task 128)
------------------------------------------------------------
Both Apple and Google strictly enforce user privacy. You must accurately declare what data you collect, why you collect it, and who you share it with.

- What we did:
  - Apple Privacy Nutrition Label: We mapped out all data points collected. 
    - Usage Data & Diagnostics (Firebase Analytics/Crashlytics) for "App Functionality" and "Analytics".
    - Contact Info (Email) for "App Functionality" (Authentication).
  - Google Play Data Safety Form: Completed the extensive questionnaire confirming that data is encrypted in transit and users can request data deletion.
  - The "On-Device AI" Advantage: Because our Ollama integration runs locally, we explicitly highlighted in our privacy policy and store descriptions that user habit data *never* leaves the device for AI processing. This is a massive selling point and simplifies our privacy compliance.

  *Interview Tip*: If asked about data privacy, emphasize the "principle of least privilege." You only collect what is strictly necessary. Highlighting the on-device AI architecture shows architectural maturity, as you intentionally designed the app to minimize PII (Personally Identifiable Information) exposure.

3. Submit for Review (Task 129)
-------------------------------
The final step is pushing the build to the stores and triggering the review process.

- What we did:
  - iOS App Store (App Store Connect): 
    - Selected the verified TestFlight build.
    - Answered the Export Compliance questionnaire (confirming we use standard industry encryption via HTTPS).
    - Set the release type to "Manual Release" so we can coordinate the exact launch time for both platforms.
    - Submitted to Apple for Review. (Initial reviews typically take 24-48 hours).
  - Android Play Store (Google Play Console):
    - Promoted the `.aab` (Android App Bundle) from the Closed Testing track to the Production track.
    - Completed the Content Rating questionnaire (rated E for Everyone/3+).
    - Submitted for Google's review.

  *Interview Tip*: Mention "Manual Release" vs "Automatic Release." Releasing manually gives you control over your launch day (Day 45). If you choose Automatic, Apple might approve it at 3 AM on a Sunday, ruining a coordinated Product Hunt or social media launch.
