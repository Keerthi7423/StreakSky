[3. TECHNICAL INTERVIEW PREPARATION]
---

### Q1: How did you implement the multi-timeframe graph switching?
**Answer**: I used a `TabBarView` combined with a `TabController` in a `StatefulWidget`. Each tab child is a `DetailedLineChart` widget passing different data lists (`weeklyTrend`, `monthlyTrend`, `careerTrend`). To ensure the transition felt "premium," I enabled animations in the `LineChart` itself so that the lines morph from one state to another rather than simply jumping between views.

### Q2: Explain the logic behind the "Keystone Habit" correlation.
**Answer**: In a behavioral app, correlation is calculated by looking at the presence of Habit A (Keystone) vs. the completion rate of all other habits on the same day. If the presence of Habit A consistently coincides with a higher overall completion rate, it has a high positive correlation. This provides actionable insight: "If you do this one thing, you're likely to win the whole day."

### Q3: Why use `fl_chart` instead of drawing custom paths?
**Answer**: `fl_chart` is the industry standard for Flutter because it provides highly optimized rendering for large datasets and built-in support for complex interactions like tooltips and touch gestures. For a "Dark & Powerful" aesthetic, I customized it by disabling borders, using custom grid lines, and adding area gradients beneath the lines to create a "neon glow" effect that matches our design system.

### Q4: How do you handle performance when rendering 365+ days of data on a line chart?
**Answer**: For the "Monthly" and "Career" views, we use "Interval Tuning" on the titles and grid. Instead of showing 30 individual labels on the X-axis (which would cause overlap), we use logic like `index % 5 == 0` to show every 5th day. Additionally, we disable individual "dots" on the line for denser datasets to keep the UI clean and the frame rate high.

---
[4. ARCHITECTURAL DECISIONS]
---
1. **Controller Pattern**: Logic for aggregating data is kept in `stats_controller.dart` (Riverpod Provider). This keeps the UI widgets "dumb" and focused only on rendering.
2. **Component Reusability**: The `DetailedLineChart` was designed as a generic wrapper. It takes `accentColor` as a parameter, allowing different timeframes to have different "moods" (Green for Weekly, Blue for Monthly, Purple for Career).
3. **Route Nesting**: In `app_router.dart`, `GraphsScreen` is a sub-route of `/stats`. This maintains a logical navigation hierarchy where "Deep Analytics" is a specialized view of "General Stats."
