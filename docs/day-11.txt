3. TECHNICAL BREAKDOWN (INTERVIEW PREP)
---------------------------------------

Q: How do you handle complex habit frequencies like "3 times per week"?
A: In StreakSky, I implemented an `isDue` method that checks the `FrequencyType`. For "times per week", the logic checks the total completions for the current week. If the count is less than the target, the habit remains "due" every day until the goal is met. This ensures the user is consistently prompted until they fulfill their weekly commitment.

Q: What is the benefit of the "Habit Heartbeat"?
A: It's a UX micro-interaction that provides immediate psychological feedback. By using haptics on app open, we tell the user their status (on track vs. behind) without them even looking at the screen. This builds a somatic connection with the app, making the habit-tracking experience feel more visceral and "alive".

Q: Explain your Offline-First strategy for habit completion.
A: We use Hive for local storage and Supabase for remote storage. When a user toggles a habit, we first save it to Hive and then trigger a background sync. This ensures zero latency and works perfectly in poor network conditions. We also use a Firestore realtime listener to ensure cross-device synchronization.

Q: How do you handle "Soft Deletion" (Archiving)?
A: Instead of deleting records from the database, we use an `is_archived` boolean flag. This is critical for habit trackers because even if a user stops a habit, we want to preserve their historical heatmap and streak data. In the UI, we filter out archived habits using providers like `habitsListProvider.where((h) => !h.isArchived)`.
