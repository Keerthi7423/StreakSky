1. TASK 115: Refine Haptics (Medium Impact & Success Pattern)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Context:
Haptic feedback is crucial for a tactile, satisfying user experience. It provides physical confirmation of digital actions.

Implementation Details:
- Checkbox Haptics: In `habit_card.dart`, we ensure that toggling a habit triggers a medium impact haptic feedback (`HapticFeedback.mediumImpact()`). Medium impact provides a solid, noticeable click without being overpowering, ideal for frequent actions like completing a daily habit.
- Milestone Success Haptics: In `streak_controller.dart`, when a user hits a new milestone (e.g., 7 days, 30 days), we trigger `HapticService.milestoneSuccess()`. This custom pattern (Light -> Medium -> Heavy impact) creates a crescendo effect, physically mimicking a celebration to reward the user's consistency.

Interview Talking Points:
- "We used varying levels of haptic feedback depending on the importance of the action. A standard habit completion gets a medium impact, while hitting a 30-day streak milestone triggers a multi-step success pattern to provide positive physical reinforcement."
- "This creates an 'Epoxy-style' satisfying interaction that keeps users coming back."

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. TASK 116: Polish Typography Hierarchy (Tabular Figures)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Context:
In an app with many changing numbers (streak counts, statistics, percentages), standard proportional fonts can cause the text to "jitter" or jump horizontally when numbers change (because a '1' is narrower than an '8').

Implementation Details:
- We updated `app_typography.dart` to include `fontFeatures: [FontFeature.tabularFigures()]` across all `TextStyle` definitions.
- Tabular figures force all numbers to have the exact same width (like a monospaced font, but only for numbers).

Interview Talking Points:
- "To prevent UI jitter on our statistics and streak screens when numbers incremented, I enabled tabular figures (`FontFeature.tabularFigures()`) across our design system's typography."
- "This ensures that all numeric characters take up the exact same horizontal space, maintaining clean alignment in leaderboards and stat grids without sacrificing the aesthetic of our chosen proportional font (Inter)."

Implementation Details:

Interview Talking Points:
